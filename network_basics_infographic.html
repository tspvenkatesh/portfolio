Understanding Network Basics

The Internet is like a Road System

1. Bandwidth
🛣️
More Lanes = More Data
Bandwidth is the maximum amount of data that can travel through a connection at one time, measured in Mbps or Gbps.
Like a highway with multiple lanes—more lanes allow more cars to travel simultaneously.
2. Latency
⏱️
Travel Time Matters
Latency is the delay between sending and receiving data, measured in milliseconds. Low latency = fast response.
Like the time it takes for a car to drive from point A to point B.
3. Packets
📦
Data in Small Packages
Data is broken into small chunks called packets, each with header information and destination details.
Like shipping containers on trucks—each packet is a container carrying data to its destination.
4. Routers
🔀
Traffic Control
Routers direct packets to their destination by examining addresses and choosing optimal paths through the network.
Like traffic intersections that direct cars along the best route to their destination.
5. Congestion
🚦
Network Bottlenecks
When too much traffic uses the same path, packets queue up and experience delays.
Like rush hour traffic jams—too many cars on the same road causes slowdowns.
6. Signal Quality
📶
Clear Signal Path
Signal degradation, interference, or physical obstacles reduce data transmission reliability and speed.
Like a radio station's broadcast quality—interference weakens the signal.
7. Throughput
🎯
Actual Data Rate
Throughput is the real-world amount of data successfully transmitted, often less than theoretical bandwidth.
Like actual cars per hour passing a checkpoint—less than the road's maximum capacity.
8. Protocols
📋
Rules of Communication
Protocols are standardized rules that devices follow to communicate, ensuring compatibility and reliable data exchange.
Like traffic rules—everyone follows the same conventions for safe, orderly communication.
